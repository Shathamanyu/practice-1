Rules to Create Password

Rules
  • Here are some rules to follow when creating a strong password:
    1. Use at least 10 characters, but 12 or more is better.
    2. Use a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. You can also include nonsensical character arrangements. Avoid using guessable character substitutions or keyboard paths, like < or >.
    3. Don't use your name, birthdate, or other personal information. You can also avoid using words that are in the dictionary, or names of people, characters, products, or organizations
    4. Create a unique password for each account, device, application, website, and software. If a cyber criminal guesses one of your passwords, they can use it to try to hack into all of your accounts.
    5. You can use phrases that you can twist, like song lyrics with numbers and symbols.
